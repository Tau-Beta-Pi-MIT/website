  1. Saturday, December 10, 2005
    Professor Ellen Harris

    Ellen T. Harris, Professor of Music, Ph.D., The University of Chicago, is a musicologist working in the area of Baroque opera and vocal performance practice with a special emphasis on the music of Handel. Her most recent book, Handel as Orpheus: Voice and Desire in the Chamber Cantatas (Harvard University Press, 2001) won the Kinkeldey Award from the American Musicological Society and the Gottschalk Prize from the American Society for Eighteenth-Century Studies. Her most recent publications include "Mozart's Mitridate: Going beyond the Text" (2004) on the use and composition of ornamentation based on her work for the Santa Fe Opera, and "Handel the Investor" (2004) on Handel's finances as revealed in the records of the Bank of England. She has received fellowships from the Council for Research in the Humanities at Columbia University, the American Council of Learned Societies and the National Endowment for the Humanities. For the 1995-96 academic year, she was a Fellow at the Mary Ingraham Bunting Institute of Radcliffe College at Harvard University; in 1998 she was elected a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences; in spring 2004 she was in residence as a member of the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey. From 1989 to 1996 she served as Associate Provost for the Arts at MIT.
